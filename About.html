Hobbies

Video Games

When I have free time, I tend to spend most of it playing video games. At the moment, the game I mainly play is SMITE, but throw in some other games like Rocket League, Valorant, and Call of Duty at times. I have been a gamer my entire life, starting on the Nintendo 64 and Gameboy with Pokemon.

Once I got a PS3 I started to play Call of Duty. My first Call of Duty game was, Call of Duty III. I only played the campaign for this game, and did not play any multiplayer for Call of Duty until Modern Warfare 2 came out. I did not play Modern Warfare 2 online very much, but the next Call of Duty to be released was Call of Duty Black Ops, and I grinded the online part. Every Call of Duty I would play through the campaign to start, and then play the multiplayer part. Occassionally I would play zombies with my friends and try to complete easter eggs.

Over the many years of playing Call of Duty, I began to sharpen my skills and be a top player. Once Call of Duty Ghosts came out in 2013, I began to play competitively. I found a group of guys that had a similar mind and skillset as me to form a team. We would practice just about everyday, and have a scrimmage against other teams three to four times a week. On weekends we would play in tournaments that were mostly free to enter, but some required a fee to enter. We ended up being a decent team, but once Call of Duty: Advanced Warfare came out, we fell apart because the competitive scene was mainly on Xbox, while we all played on PS3.

After another year, the competitive scene moved to Playstation, so I bought myself a PS4. Being on many different teams for the different Call of Duty games, it really enhanced my teamwork and understanding for others because I played with people of all different kind of personalities. During Modern Warfare in 2019, I found a group of guys that were very cool, calm, and collected. We teamed up and ended up playing a tournament almost everyday. Some of these tournaments we played against the professional players that play on the big screen for millions of dollars. One time we ended up beating a team with three professional players in a wager match.

Paintball

I am currently a member of the Michigan State Paintball Team. I originally started playing paintball in 2016 for fun. I generally would only play once or twice a year, but joined MSU's paintball team my sophmore year (2018) and have been loving every second of it. We hold practices every-other week, with conditioning on the weeks we do not have practice. We are a speedball team, meaning our games are extremely fast-paced, and generally over within two minutes! We play these speedball games in tournaments, and the teams are either a five-verse-five, or three-verse-three. Imagine ten people on a 40-50 yard field firing fully automatic paintball guns. It gets very hectic right away, and that is why the rounds usually last less than two minutes.
